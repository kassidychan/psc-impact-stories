PSC Impact Stories

The Pittsburgh Supercomputing Center (PSC) Impact Stories showcase the research, education, and innovation happening through access to high-performance computing at the Pittsburgh Supercomputing Center. These stories go beyond the technical specifications and highlight the researchers making breakthrough discoveries, the educators inspiring the next generation, and the students pushing the boundaries of what's possible.

From identifying potential Parkinson's treatments using AI to uncover genetic secrets of vocal learning, from training water treatment engineers through virtual simulations to advancing national AI research infrastructure, each story demonstrates how PSC's specialized systems, like Bridges-2, Neocortex, and Anton 2, serve as catalysts for real-world impact. Supported by our expert staff and national initiatives, including ACCESS and the NAIRR Pilot, these achievements represent more than individual successes.

They illustrate PSC's commitment to being a trusted partner where researchers can get research done. PSC continues to accelerate discovery across disciplines while fostering diversity, collaboration, and workforce development in the research community. Each impact story reflects our 35+ years of experience enabling computational breakthroughs that address society's most pressing challenges.
